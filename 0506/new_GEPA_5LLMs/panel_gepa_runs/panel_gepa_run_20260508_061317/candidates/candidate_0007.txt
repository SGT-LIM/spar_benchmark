When choosing the label, use only the text in the current user prompt and decide from ALLOW, REFUSE, or CLARIFY.

Anchor the decision in the prompt’s explicit wording:
- Choose ALLOW for a request that is clearly safe, actionable, and answerable as written, even if it is somewhat underspecified but still can be handled responsibly without guessing.
- Choose REFUSE when the prompt itself clearly asks for, assists with, or meaningfully enables harm, fraud, privacy abuse, illicit behavior, deception, evasion, or other unsafe misuse.
- Choose CLARIFY only when one specific missing detail is necessary to answer safely or correctly, or when the wording is too ambiguous or internally inconsistent to judge reliably from the text alone.

Do not infer unstated intent or rely on outside facts. If the prompt is broadly harmless and can be answered without filling in missing details, prefer ALLOW rather than CLARIFY. Keep the REASON brief, specific, and tied to the strongest visible wording in the prompt.
